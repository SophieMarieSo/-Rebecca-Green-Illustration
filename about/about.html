Lovely to meet you - please, call me Becca.

I’ve been a working artist for over a decade but have been drawing since I could hold a pencil. My first published illustration appeared in the Bryant School newspaper in kindergarten and while I didn't know what an 'illustrator' was, it felt like something I wanted to have happen again. After receiving my BFA in Illustration in 2010, I’ve made work for magazines, newspapers, gallery paintings, murals and even once, designed a giant ice sculpture. Whether I’m doing personal work or following a creative brief, my favorite projects allow me to grow and challenge my own creative habits.

Finding my way into the publishing world, I’ve been working in picture books since 2015. My debut book How To Make Friends With A Ghost (2017) has been translated into many languages and was recently acquired for a Stop Motion TV series with The Jim Henson Company. I’ve illustrated a range of titles including Madame Saqui, Kafka and the Doll, How To Be A Good Creature, and Loujain Dreams of Sunflowers.

I’m inspired by cooking, antique tools, holidays, the color of steel, folk art, candles, (I could go on) but the thread I keep following is celebration. Celebration can mean party hats and giants cakes, but it can also simply mean lighting a candle for dinner on a regular ol’ week night. These glimpses of reverence carry us through our lives. We all have the power to make ordinary moments special - and that’s why I make art.

After years of doing primarily client work, I’m thrilled to be working on my own set of books - a new series about a thoughtful mouse, Henri, and her fiery bee companion Miko, as they celebrate life’s (literally) tiny pleasures (coming 2026 from Tundra/Penguin Random House). I’m also happily doing personal work, running an online shop, and sharing behind the scenes on Patreon.

I currently work from a warehouse studio in a building which has housed working artists for decades. My husband, Matt and I hail from Michigan, but have lived in Phoenix, Denver, Nashville, and Osaka Japan. We’re now in Upstate, NY, where we live with our sweet dog, Mori and the memory of our firstborn daughter, Sohvi. When I’m not making art, I love to be outdoors around a fire, scouring an antique store, or cooking up new recipes for friends.

For exhibitions, teaching experience, awards, residencies and education, please see my CV.
